Platinum Sponsors

Ford

Ford Motor Company has put the world on wheels since its founding in 1903 by Henry Ford. Ford, one of the world’s largest automakers operating in over 125 countries around the world, sells automobiles and commercial vehicles under the Ford brand, and luxury cars under its Lincoln brand. Ford’s automobiles, innovations, and manufacturing have made the blue oval known around the world since its founding.

Michigan Engineering

Michigan Engineering provides scientific and technological leadership to the people of the world. We seek to improve the quality of life by developing intellectually curious and socially conscious minds, creating collaborative solutions to societal problems, and promoting an inclusive and innovative community of service for the common good.

Nissan

Nissan Motor Corporation, stylized as Nissan, is a global automaker with headquarters in Yokohama, Japan. Founded in 1933, Nissan has transitioned from its early days to a major player in the automotive industry, known for its innovative and eco-friendly vehicles. Nissan's product line includes electric cars, sports cars, SUVs, and commercial vehicles, reflecting its commitment to sustainability and advanced technology. Employing over 137,000 people worldwide, Nissan operates in numerous countries, embodying a legacy of quality and innovation in automotive engineering.

STMicroelectronics

STMicroelectronics, commonly known as ST, is a leading semiconductor company based in Geneva, Switzerland. Established in 1987 from the merger of two semiconductor entities, ST has evolved into a key player in the electronics sector, offering a broad array of microcontrollers, sensors, and semiconductors for automotive, industrial, and IoT applications. With a workforce of over 46,000 globally, ST is dedicated to driving smart, eco-friendly technologies across the electronics landscape, emphasizing innovation, sustainability, and customer-centric solutions in its operations.
